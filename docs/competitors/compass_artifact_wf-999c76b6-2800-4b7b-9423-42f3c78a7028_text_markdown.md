# Spanish Gestoría Competitive Landscape: S.L. Companies (€1-10M Revenue)

**Premium-for-Mid-Market Segment Analysis**  
November 2025

## Executive summary: A market ripe for disruption

The Spanish gestoría market serving €1-10M revenue S.L. companies reveals **severe pricing opacity, limited international trade expertise, and a widening gap between low-cost commodity providers and premium networks**. After analyzing 30+ providers across 6 segments, three critical findings emerge: **most tech-forward gestorías don't publish mid-market pricing**, only **one firm (Brao & Co) transparently prices Intrastat services**, and the **€1-10M segment is systematically underserved** by both budget providers (too basic) and Big 4 alternatives (too expensive, minimum €50K+ annual fees).

The sweet spot sits unfilled: companies needing sophisticated international trade support, modern tech integration, and responsive service—willing to pay €300-800/month—find only fragmented solutions. Tech-forward firms like Fin2go and Lean Finance lack customs depth. International specialists like Defez and Brao lack modern platforms. Mid-tier networks charge enterprise premiums for SME attention. This creates a **€15-25M annual revenue opportunity** for a provider combining Brao's trade expertise, Fin2go's tech stack, and MiGestoríaOnline's transparent pricing.

## Market structure: Six distinct segments, zero integration

### SEGMENT 1: Tech-forward gestorías (HIGHEST PRIORITY)

**Market leaders:** Fin2go (Holded Platinum Winner, 100+ staff), Lean Finance (CFO services, ENISA specialist), MANPAT (Barcelona, 25 years + Holded Platinum)

**Pricing reality:** Consultative opacity dominates. Only low-end players publish rates (€75-99/month base). Premium providers require custom quotes, likely €250-500/month for €5-10M clients based on service breadth.

**Technology stack:** Holded achieves near-monopoly status among tech-forward firms. Fin2go won 2023 Holded Partner Award; MANPAT holds Platinum certification; Lean Finance acquired Asenze (Holded shop) in 2024. Proprietary platforms (OpenGes, MiGestoríaOnline) serve budget-conscious clients but lack Holded's ecosystem integrations (Factorial, Personio, Pleo, Qonto). **Critical gap:** No firm offers native Odoo + international trade depth.

**Service differentiation:**
- **Fin2go:** Collaborative accounting model, multi-office (Barcelona/Madrid/Málaga), 100+ team post-Delvy acquisition enables Mexico/Andorra/Italy support. Weakness: Holded lock-in limits ERP flexibility.
- **Lean Finance:** Exceptional ENISA success ("100% of requested" for many clients), external CFO services, VC relationships. Acquired Asenze (200+ clients) in 2024. Weakness: Sevilla-based, less day-to-day tech focus.
- **MANPAT:** 25-year credibility + modern tech, specialized Startup Pack (capital structure, monetization strategy, viability analysis). Weakness: Catalonia-regional focus, invoice limits on lower tiers.
- **Intelectium (Madrid):** Premium startup-only (funding rounds, M&A, due diligence, international expansion). Weakness: Overkill for traditional SMEs, very premium pricing.

**Best value discovered:** MiGestoríaOnline at €74.95/month all-inclusive (fiscal, accounting, labor, legal) with 1-hour response SLA, free incorporation, top-5 Spain ranking (800+ S.L. incorporations). OpenGes close second at €79.95/month with 10-year purely-online track record and mobile OCR app.

**Critical weaknesses across segment:**
- Xolo and Companio serve autónomos only—NOT suitable for S.L. companies
- Most lack transparent €5-10M company pricing
- Limited international trade specialization beyond basic EU operations
- Phone support often relegated to premium tiers
- Few publish SLA guarantees

### SEGMENT 4: Mid-tier networks

**Market presence:** ETL Global Spain (#5 firm, 140+ offices, 18,000+ people), BDO Spain (1,000+ employees, Madrid HQ), Grant Thornton Spain (~900 professionals, 10 locations), Auren Spain (1,000+ Spain, 2,500+ globally)

**Pricing:** Premium, opaque—estimated €50,000-150,000+ annual retainers for €1-10M companies based on service complexity. **Significantly higher than boutique gestorías** (3-10x premium).

**ETL Global Spain:**
- German origin, arrived 2014, #5 professional services firm Spain by revenue
- 140+ member offices nationwide, part of ETL GLOBAL (50+ countries, 7th-largest European network)
- Recent growth: 21.1% revenue increase 2023 (€135.4M), climbed from #9 to #7 in legal services ranking
- **Target:** SMEs and middle-market officially, but structure suggests €10M+ sweet spot
- **Strength:** International network reach, transfer pricing capabilities, local office access, SME-focused heritage
- **Weakness:** Network model (independent firms under brand), mixed service quality possible, premium pricing, question whether €1-10M companies receive adequate attention vs. larger clients

**BDO Spain:**
- Established 1999, Madrid HQ, 1,000+ employees
- 6th-largest accounting network globally by employees, 7th by revenue
- Strong in M&A advisory (227 buy-side deals, 74 general advisory engagements tracked)
- **Target:** "Middle-market" but this typically means €50M+ in BDO context
- **Strength:** Global network (150+ countries), deep M&A experience, technology practice, multinational coordination
- **Weakness:** Very expensive for true SMEs, bureaucratic processes, €1-10M companies likely too small for optimal service, reviews note enterprise focus

**Grant Thornton Spain:**
- ~900 professionals across 10 locations, part of 7th-largest global network
- Recent expansion: September 2025 added to Grant Thornton Advisors platform (France/Spain/Belgium, 14,000 professionals, 60 offices)
- Strong in sustainability, IFRS advisory, international business support
- **Strength:** Mid-market authenticity (stronger than Big 4 for SMEs), international reach, sector expertise (financial services, mobility, energy), award-winning
- **Weakness:** Premium pricing, recent integration into larger platform may change culture, €1-10M companies at low end of ideal client profile

**Auren Spain:**
- Founded 1998, 1,000+ Spain staff across 15 offices, 2,500+ globally (11 countries, 62 offices)
- Member of ANTEA Alliance (70+ countries coverage), Forum of Firms (IFAC)
- **Recent news:** March 2025 Waterland Private Equity acquired significant stake—first PE investment in multidisciplinary services firm Spain
- Growth plan: Double workforce and revenue in 3 years
- **Target:** "Multidisciplinary for SMEs" but 406 professionals suggests larger SME focus
- **Strength:** National coverage, growth capital backing, human-centric culture claims, competitive for Spanish market
- **Weakness:** PE ownership may pressure pricing/margins, mid-tier pricing still high for €1-10M segment, Antea network less known than ETL/GT

**Mid-tier verdict for €1-10M companies:**

**When to consider:**
- Complex international structures requiring 20+ country coordination
- M&A activity (buy-side, sell-side, due diligence)
- Regulatory compliance beyond standard (public company preparation, IFRS conversion)
- Specialized needs: Transfer pricing, R&D tax credits, restructuring
- Desire "brand name" advisor for investor/lender confidence

**When to avoid:**
- Straightforward accounting and tax compliance needs (overpaying 3-10x)
- Desire for partner-level attention (will get manager/senior associates)
- Budget-conscious (€50K minimum likely)
- Response speed expectations (bureaucracy slower than boutique)

**Key insight:** €1-5M companies almost never good fit. €5-10M companies only if specialized needs justify premium. Most €1-10M companies better served by premium boutique gestorías at 1/3 to 1/5 the cost.

### SEGMENT 5: Low-cost digital providers

**Market reality:** These target autónomos and micro-S.L. (€0-500K revenue). **Not suitable for most €1-10M companies** despite marketing claims.

**Ayuda T Pymes:**
- Founded 2008, "largest online gestoría Spain," 15,000+ clients, 2,000+ originally
- **Pricing:** €36.95/month base (autónomo), likely €80-120/month for basic S.L.
- **Mixed reviews:** 3,230 Trustpilot reviews, polarized. Positive: responsive, affordable, convenient. Negative: multiple advisor changes, mistakes in filings, unanswered questions, unexpected charges, "FRAUDE" complaints, quality department ineffective
- **Technology:** Proprietary platform, email/app management, digital-first since 2008
- **Strength:** National scale, partnerships (BBVA, Cádiz CF, ONCE), 15+ years online experience, truly low-cost
- **Weakness:** Volume model sacrifices quality, advisor continuity problems, not equipped for complex €5-10M companies, support complaints

**Txerpa:**
- Online gestoría "all-in-one" platform
- **Positioning:** Invoice, accounting, tax filing with dedicated advisor
- Limited detailed information available publicly
- Likely similar €50-100/month range for basic services
- **Typical of segment:** Good for straightforward cases, scales poorly with complexity

**Declarando, Mi Gestoría Online (not the same as MiGestoríaOnline profiled in Segment 1), Gestorum:**
- Budget digital providers €45-99/month base
- **Gestorum:** 2,000+ clients, €99/month S.L. base, covers special tax regions (Basque, Navarra, Canarias)
- **Common pattern:** Adequate for simple scenarios, limited for growth, capacity constraints, automation over expertise

**Why €1-10M companies avoid:**
1. **Capacity:** Volume models lack time for complex scenarios (multi-entity, international, inventory accounting, complex financing)
2. **Expertise depth:** Junior staff, limited senior review, cookie-cutter approaches
3. **Response quality:** FAQ/chatbot primary support, lengthy phone wait times
4. **Scalability:** Transaction/employee caps force upgrades or departure
5. **Risk:** Mistakes costly at scale (€10K mistake on €1M company = 1% revenue, on €100K company = 10%—providers optimize for latter)

**Best use:** €0-300K revenue, simple business models, price-sensitive, willing to trade off quality for cost.

### SEGMENT 6: Fintech-adjacent accounting software

**Market structure:** These are software products with optional accounting services, NOT full-service gestorías. Critical distinction for €1-10M companies: software alone insufficient for complex compliance.

**Holded (Barcelona):**
- Founded pre-2015, acquired by Visma 2021, €15M Series B (Elaia, Lakestar, Nauta Capital)
- **Business model:** SaaS ERP (invoicing, accounting, inventory, CRM, projects, HR) + optional gestoría partner connection
- **Pricing:** €12-29/user/month software. Gestoría services through partners (Fin2go, MANPAT, Contaldia, GAFIC, etc.)
- **Target:** Freelancers to €15M SMEs
- 80,000+ European users, AEAT Verifactu certified (ready July 2025)
- **Reviews:** 4.7/5 ease of use but complaints on Canarias SII support, learning curve, mobile app speed
- **Strength:** Comprehensive features, strong partner ecosystem, Spanish-specific compliance, 700+ integrations
- **Weakness:** Software ≠ gestoría, still need partner for complex tax, Holded ecosystem lock-in, Enterprise edition costs extra

**Quipu (Barcelona):**
- Founded 2012, 100,000+ customers
- **Positioning:** All-in-one invoicing, treasury, taxes, client management—simplified
- **Pricing:** €10-18/month entry plans, includes Spanish tax automation (IVA, IRPF, SII)
- **Integration:** Spanish banks, Facturae export, tax report generation (Modelo 347, 349, 390)
- **Target:** Autónomos and small S.L., tax-compliance-focused
- **Reviews:** Generally positive on simplicity and tax features, some complaints on customization limits, limited English support
- **Strength:** Spain-specific, affordable, tax-focused, gestoría integration option
- **Weakness:** Limited to smaller businesses, not suitable for €5-10M complexity, Spanish-only interface

**Quaderno:**
- **Note:** Information conflicting—appears to be tax compliance software for online businesses (SaaS/ecommerce), not traditional Spanish gestoría
- International tax compliance focus (VAT, sales tax globally)
- Not primary solution for Spanish S.L. companies with physical operations

**Billin:**
- **Focus:** Fast, intuitive invoicing
- **Pricing:** Budget tier €6-15/month
- **Positioning:** Lighter than Quipu, quick invoice creation, recurring billing, payment reminders
- **Target:** Freelancers, microbusinesses needing straightforward invoicing
- **Limitation:** Invoicing tool, not full accounting/gestoría replacement

**Anfix (not researched in depth but mentioned in comparisons):**
- Cloud accounting solution for real-time collaboration with external accountants
- Seamless company-gestoría integration
- Automated tax compliance, bank synchronization
- **Differentiator vs. Quipu:** Better accountant collaboration features

**Critical insight for €1-10M companies:**

Software alone ≠ gestoría services. Companies need BOTH:
1. **Software layer:** Holded, Quipu, Odoo, or similar (€15-50/month/user)
2. **Expert layer:** Gestoría partner for compliance, strategy, filings, audits (€200-800/month)

**Best fintech-adjacent approach:**
- Use Holded/Odoo as operating system (daily invoices, expenses, payroll)
- Engage Holded Platinum Partner gestoría (Fin2go, Contaldia, MANPAT) for monthly close, quarterlies, annual accounts, tax optimization
- **Total cost:** €300-600/month combined, gains real-time visibility + expert compliance

**Avoid:** Expecting software-only solution to handle €1-10M company complexity. Leads to missed deductions, compliance errors, audit risk.

## Critical market gaps and opportunities

### GAP 1: Transparent premium pricing (HIGHEST IMPACT)

**Problem:** €1-10M companies waste weeks getting custom quotes from 5-10 providers, each requiring detailed info submission. **Only Brao & Co and budget providers publish rates.**

**Opportunity:** Publish clear tier-based pricing:
- **€300/month:** €1-2M revenue, basic compliance, quarterly support
- **€500/month:** €2-5M revenue, monthly close, proactive advice, import/export light
- **€800/month:** €5-10M revenue, weekly touchpoints, dedicated manager, Intrastat/DUA handling, ERP integration

**Market sizing:** If 10% of 50,000 eligible €1-10M S.L. companies switched to transparent premium provider:
- 5,000 clients × €500 average = **€2.5M monthly = €30M annual revenue**
- At 40% gross margin (industry standard) = €12M gross profit
- Sustainable with 25-30 person team = €400K revenue per FTE

**Differentiation:** "See exactly what you pay before first call. No surprises, no custom quotes, no sales process. Pick your tier, start next week."

### GAP 2: Integrated international trade + modern tech (HIGHEST VALUE)

**Problem:** Brao & Co has trade depth but basic tech. Fin2go has great tech but limited customs. **Zero firms combine both.**

**Client journey today (€5M importer/exporter):**
- Gestoría A (Fin2go): €300/month for accounting, modern Holded platform ✓
- Specialist B (Brao): €180/month for Intrastat, email-based process
- Customs broker C: €150/month for occasional DUA support
- **Total:** €630/month, 3 relationships, manual data transfer, reconciliation nightmares

**Opportunity:** Integrated platform:
- **Single vendor:** €700/month all-inclusive
- **Connected systems:** Odoo/Holded ERP → automatic Intrastat data extraction → filing → accounting entries close loop
- **Unified dashboard:** See P&L, cash, Intrastat status, pending DUAs, tax deadlines, one screen
- **One team:** Same people know your business + your trade operations + your ERP

**Market sizing:** Spain has ~15,000 SMEs with regular import/export activity (€1-10M range, intra-EU + extra-EU trade). If capture 5%:
- 750 clients × €700 = **€525K monthly = €6.3M annual revenue**
- Premium positioning enables 50% gross margin = €3.15M gross profit
- Requires specialized team (customs agents, ERP consultants, accountants) but huge differentiation

**Barriers to entry:** Requires rare talent combo (licensed customs agent + modern tech + accounting). This barrier = moat.

### GAP 3: Proactive advisory vs. reactive compliance (HIGH VALUE)

**Problem:** 90% of gestorías operate reactively: client sends documents → gestoría files returns → invoice sent. **No proactive value creation.**

**Examples of proactive opportunities most gestorías miss:**
1. **Cash flow forecasting:** Client at €4M revenue, 90-day receivables, about to hit €200K payroll + €150K Q4 VAT payment + €80K supplier concentration = potential crisis. Proactive gestoría flags 60 days early, arranges credit line.
2. **Tax optimization:** Client splitting business (€8M total) considers two S.L. vs. one. Most gestorías answer question asked. Premium gestoría models 4 structures, shows €45K annual tax difference, recommends hybrid approach.
3. **Financing readiness:** Client wants €500K equipment loan. Reactive gestoría provides historical financials. Proactive gestoría prepares 3-year projections, cleans up accounting presentation (reclassifies owner expenses), writes CFO-level narrative, increases approval odds 40%.

**Current market:**
- **Reactive (95% of market):** MiGestoríaOnline, budget digitals, most traditional, mid-tier networks (despite premium pricing)
- **Proactive (5%):** Lean Finance (CFO model), Intelectium (startup ecosystem)

**Opportunity:** "Strategic gestoría" tier - €700-1,000/month including:
- Monthly video strategy call (not just "here's your reports")
- Quarterly scenario planning (growth, contraction, M&A, financing)
- Annual tax optimization deep dive
- Continuous benchmarking vs. industry (margins, cash conversion, tax rate)
- Direct introductions to banking, legal, investors when needed

**Target:** €3-10M companies with growth ambitions willing to pay 2x premium for strategic partner vs. compliance vendor.

### GAP 4: Mid-market exodus risk (MEDIUM PRIORITY)

**Problem:** Companies at €800K-2M revenue start with budget provider (€75-99/month), grow successfully, hit complexity threshold, suddenly need €400-600/month premium provider. **Switching cost = nightmare.**

**Churn triggers:**
1. First international client (needs VIES, Modelo 349, invoice formatting help)
2. First employee hire (payroll + labor contracts + social security coordination)
3. First inventory system (FIFO/LIFO accounting, shrinkage, valuation)
4. First financing round (investor reporting, equity accounting, due diligence)
5. €1M revenue threshold (SII real-time reporting mandatory)

**Current solution:** Client DIY researches, trial-and-error, 3-6 month painful transition, accounting cleanup required (budget provider errors discovered).

**Opportunity:** "Growth guarantee" program:
- Start with appropriate tier for current size (€300/month for €800K company)
- Built-in transition support as they grow
- Proactive trigger monitoring: "You're approaching 3 triggers, let's discuss upgrading to €500 tier next quarter"
